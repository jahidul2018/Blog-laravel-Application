<section class="text-description">
<p>The 14-year-old girl was pronouced dead at the scene after the school mini-bus collided with a lorry on Kingsbury Road in Castle Vale, Birmingham this morning.&nbsp;</p>
<p>She has been named locally as Holly Brown who had an identical twin at the school.</p>
<p>Holly dreamed of becoming a ballet dancer and three years ago beat 250 other youngsters to win a starring role in Sleeping Beauty at Wolverhampton's Grand Theatre.</p>
<p>Tributes to the popular teenager flooded in on social media following her death.</p>
<p>Rosie May wrote: "Absolutely heartbroken for Holly Brown. To her family and friends, know that pupils from John Taylor, past and present are devastated RIP."</p>
<p>Tom Davies said: "Rest in Peace Holly Brown, thoughts are with the whole family and everybody involved..words fail me."</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Superintendent Dean Hatton, head of the Central Motorway Police Group, said: "This is a tragic incident which has resulted in the death of a pupil from the John Taylor High School in Barton-under-Needwood.</p>
<p>&ldquo;The family of the teenage girl has been informed and they are being supported by officers from our family liaison unit.</p>
<p>&ldquo;We are also in contact with the school and our colleagues at Staffordshire Police, who are understandably devastated as this occurred while their students were on a school trip.&rdquo;</p>
<p>Concerned mum Tracey Pegg said her son, who is unharmed, was on the trip.&nbsp;</p>
<p>She said: "I just spoke to my son. They are sending a minibus to pick them up and take them back to school.</p>
<p>"It's just horrible. I am going to fetch him from there and bring him straight home but we don't know the full details yet. The traffic around the area is horrendous."</p>
</section>

<section class="text-description">
<p>A West Midlands Ambulance Service spokesman said: &ldquo;Crews arrived on scene to discover a collision between a minibus and a lorry.</p>
<p>&ldquo;There were 25 patients, from the minibus. One of whom, a teenage girl, had suffered serious injuries in the collision and unfortunately it immediately became apparent that nothing could be done to save her and she was confirmed dead at the scene.</p>
<p>&ldquo;A second teenage girl received treatment at the scene for minor injuries and was transported to Heartlands Hospital for further assessment.</p>
<p>&ldquo;A further 19 children were discharged at the scene together with four adults, which included the minibus driver.</p>
<p>&ldquo;The lorry driver, a man, was also assessed and discharged at the scene with having avoided injury.&rdquo;</p>
